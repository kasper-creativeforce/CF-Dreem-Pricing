Frequently asked questions.

Looking for more information? Check out these frequently asked questions about our pricing.

Do we need to commit to a minimum contract length?

Our standard contract requires a 12-month commitment. This gives you steady, reliable access to all our tools and support. It also allows us to work closely with your team, understand your needs, and adapt as your business grows over time.

What if we need to upgrade/downgrade our plan?

Your commitment to a specific plan and number of assets is locked for the duration of the contract period. However, you can always purchase additional assets as needed, benefiting from a tiered pricing structure where the cost per asset decreases with higher volumes.

Downgrades to a lower plan are not available during the active subscription period. However, at each contract renewal, you have the option to adjust your commitment by selecting a lower plan with fewer assets. This ensures flexibility in scaling up as needed while maintaining clarity and consistency in your contracted commitment.

Can we switch to a higher plan at any time?

Of course! You can upgrade your plan anytime. We'll refund the unused part of your current plan, so you only pay for the time you used the lower plan. This makes switching to a higher tier at a lower cost easy. Upgrading is simple, fast, and designed to support your growing needs.

Do you offer a free trial?

We don't offer a free trial, but we do provide personalized demos to show how Creative Force fits your needs. Our team will walk you through our platform, highlighting the features most relevant to your business and answer any questions you might have. To learn more about how Creative Force can help your business, book a demo with one of our experts.

How do you define assets?

We define an asset as the output of the last step in a workflow. This ensures that only finalized and delivered outputs are counted — regardless of the number of intermediate steps or reworks. For example, if your team manages a photography project and uploads 100 raw images during the capture stage, but only 10 are selected for post-production and final delivery, only the 10 delivered images are counted as assets. (Remember, we estimate price based on the number of final assets your team projects it will deliver annually.)

What are Dreem AI credits, and how do they work? NEW

Dreem AI credits are the currency for generating virtual content inside Creative Force. Each AI action — a virtual model image, a packshot, a product video, or an image refinement — uses a specific number of credits. Credits are purchased separately from your CF plan as an annual add-on, with a custom commitment matched to your expected volume. Price per credit ranges from $0.16 down to $0.06, scaling with the volume you commit to. Billing is consolidated with your existing Creative Force agreement.

What's the difference between assets and credits? NEW

Your CF platform fee is based on delivered assets — the final images and videos that pass QC and reach your downstream systems. Dreem credits are a separate add-on that covers the generation cost of virtual content. The two are billed together on your CF agreement, but priced independently: you pay for the assets that ship, and you commit to the credit volume you expect to consume.

How much does it cost to generate a virtual model image? NEW

A 2K virtual model image uses 11 credits. At the enterprise volume rate of $0.06/credit that's $0.66 per image; at the entry rate of $0.16/credit it's $1.76. Your account team can walk through a cost comparison based on your actual production volumes and internal benchmarks during the scoping process.

Can we increase our credit volume as production scales? NEW

Yes. Credits are purchased through the standard CF add-on process, and your account team can expand your annual commitment at any time — no renegotiation required, no downtime. Larger commitments unlock better per-credit pricing.
