FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Looking for more information? Check out these frequently asked questions about our pricing.

Do we need to commit to a minimum subscription length?

+
All plans are on an annual subscription. This gives your team stable access to the full platform and ensures we can provide the dedicated support and onboarding enterprise teams need to succeed.

What are Dreem AI credits, and how do they work? NEW

+
Dreem AI credits are the currency for generating virtual content inside Creative Force. Each AI action — a virtual model image, a packshot, a product video, or an image refinement — uses a specific number of credits. Your monthly credits reset each billing cycle. Unused credits roll over for one month. Need more? Your account team handles it in minutes.

What's the difference between assets and credits? NEW

+
Your platform fee is based on delivered assets — the final images and videos that pass QC and reach your downstream systems. AI credits cover the generation cost of creating virtual content. An AI-generated image that passes QC counts as one delivered asset. You only pay for assets that ship; credits cover the cost of generating them.

How much does it cost to generate a virtual model image? NEW

+
A 2K virtual model image uses 11 credits, costing approximately $0.66 at enterprise annual rates. Traditional on-model photography typically runs $61–$245 per image when you account for talent, studio time, and post-production — meaning virtual production can be 92x–371x cheaper per image.

Can we increase our credit volume as production scales?

+
Yes. Your account team can expand your credit allocation at any time — no renegotiation required, no downtime. Enterprise customers can also discuss custom volume pricing for large-scale production runs.

Can we move to a higher plan at any time?

+
Yes. You can upgrade at any time during your subscription. Your account team will handle the transition.

How do you define assets?

+
An asset is a finalized, delivered output — the image, video, or file that passes QC and is delivered to your downstream systems. Intermediate files, work-in-progress, and rejected shots do not count toward your asset volume.
